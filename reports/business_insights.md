# Business Insights Report

## Executive Summary

This report summarizes the key findings from the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) performed on the Lending Club loan portfolio. The objective was to understand portfolio performance, borrower characteristics, credit risk patterns, and lending trends before moving into SQL analysis, Power BI dashboard development, and machine learning.

---

# Portfolio Overview

- Total Records: 2,260,638
- Features: 36
- Default Rate: ~13.02%
- Non-Default Rate: ~86.98%

The lending portfolio is largely composed of performing loans, indicating overall healthy portfolio performance.

---

# Customer Insights

- Middle-income borrowers represent the largest customer segment.
- Debt Consolidation is the most common loan purpose.
- Mortgage and Rent are the dominant home ownership categories.
- Most borrowers have stable employment histories.

---

# Credit Risk Insights

- Default rates increase as loan grade declines.
- Higher interest-rate groups experience higher default rates.
- Credit score remains one of the strongest indicators of borrower quality.
- Income segmentation provides additional insight into repayment behavior.

---

# Geographic Insights

- Lending activity is concentrated in a limited number of states.
- Default rates vary across states, suggesting regional differences in credit performance.

---

# Time Trends

- Loan issuance changes noticeably across years.
- Portfolio growth reflects changing lending activity over time.

---

# Overall Conclusions

1. The portfolio is financially healthy, with most loans remaining in performing status.
2. Loan grade, credit score, and interest rate are the strongest indicators of default risk.
3. Customer segmentation improves business reporting and portfolio monitoring.
4. Geographic analysis highlights opportunities for regional risk assessment.
5. These insights directly support the SQL analysis, Power BI dashboard, machine learning model, and Streamlit application developed in later phases.
